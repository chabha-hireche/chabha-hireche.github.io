About Me

I'm Chabha Hireche a Phd Student at Lab-STICC, Brest in France. Working on mission planning for UAVs. I also have the privilege of teaching the computer science in INSA-Lyon at the first cycle departement.

My aim interest during my Phd Thesis is the mission planning of autonomous vehicles such as UAVs considering the incertaintiesthat can be appears during the mission. My Thesis is part of the HPeC project (ANR 2015-2019). The HPeC project aims to demonstrate the ability of self-adaptive sysytems to process high-performance calculations, particulary in the case of autonomous UAV mission. To achieve the UAV mission, the project topic propose to dynamically configure an FPGA/SoC board to run the applications on board the UAV. This configuretaion, must satisfy some constraints like availability of calculations resources, energy, deal with uncertainties such as sensor failures, etc.

My research involves studying, implementing and integrating a probabilistic method of autonomous UAV mission control. I propose a solution that consists of using the Markov Decision Process (MDP) for decision making, and the Bayesian Networks (BN) to evaluate the system reliability (diagnosis). Generally, the both models (MDP and BN) are used separately. In the case of autonomous decision-making, we need to consider the uncertainties related to the mission context (Obstacle, wind, etc), health management of the system and its components and the availability of hardware resources needed to execute the required applications to achieve the mission. To integrate all these constraints into the mission planning process, a model combaning the MDP and the BN is proposed. Both models (MDP and BN) work in a complementary way to make the right decision that will be executed by the UAV during the mission.
